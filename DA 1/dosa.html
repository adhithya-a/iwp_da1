Ghee Roast Dosa

Why is Ghee Roast Dosa my favourite dish?

I first tried Ghee Roast Dosa as a kid in Adyar Ananda Bhavan (A2B) restaurant. They made the dosa in a cone shape, and placed a banana leaf below it, and gave us sambar and chutney as side dishes. I was impressed by their presentation of the dish, and when I tried, it tasted even better.

Ever since then, this dosa has been my favourite dish, and I always order it whenever I go to South Indian restaurants.

Ingredients: 1 ½ cups of idli rice, ½ cup urad dal, 1 tablespoon of chana dal, 1/3 cup of thick poha, 20 methi seeds, ½ tsp of salt, water and ghee.

Steps:

  1. In a bowl soak the idli rice and washed poha for about 4-5 hours.
  2. In another bowl, take urad dal, chana dal and methi seeds. Rinse and soak for the same amount of time.
  3. After grinding mix both batters together add salt and leave for 10-12 hours for fermentation.
  4. Stir the batter once before you start making your dosas.
  5. Heat ghee in a tawa on medium flame and pour the batter.
  6. Once the top side of the dosa looks done, pour ghee on top and sides of the dosa. This will make the edges crisp and golden.
  7. Fold the cooked dosa and serve with coconut chutney.
 Pretty easy, isn’t it? Try this out today!
